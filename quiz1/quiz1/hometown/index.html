Hometown

Tuban Regency is a regency in East Java which is located on the North Coast of East Java. This district with a population of around 1.2 million people consists of 20 sub-districts and thousands of cities in Tuban District. Tuban Regency has a strategic location, which is on the border of East Java and Central Java Provinces, which is crossed by Daendels National Road on the North Coast. Tuban Regency is directly adjacent to Rembang to the west, Lamongan to the east, and Bojonegoro to the south. The administrative center of Tuban Regency is located 100 km northwest of Surabaya, the capital of East Java Province and 210 km east of Semarang, the capital of Central Java Province. Therefore, in ancient times Tuban was used as the main port of the Majapahit Kingdom and became one of the centers for the spread of Islam by the Walisongo.

Tuban Regency is on the northern coast line and in the North Cretaceous mountain range. The North Limestone Mountains in Tuban stretch from Jatirogo District to Widang District, and from Merakurak District to Soko District. Meanwhile, the sea area stretches between 5 districts, namely Bancar District, Tambakboyo District, Jenu District, Tuban District and Palang District. Tuban Regency is at the northern end and western part of East Java, which is directly on the border of East Java and Central Java or between Tuban and Rembang Regencies. Tuban has the lowest point, which is 0 m asl which is on the Pantura Line and the highest point is 500 m which is located in the Grabagan District. Tuban is also traversed by the Bengawan Solo River which flows from Solo to Gresik.
